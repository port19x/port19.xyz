~1 year update on my spending habits

Where we left off

So in July of last year, following a particularly egregious impulse purchase, I started to curb my spending by following a new system. I call it the rolling shopping list, and have been using it as a tool to stop impulse purchases with great effect. Yet, it hasn't been the silver bullet to solve all spending issues that I had wished it to be.

Needless shopping

While I've been largely consistent in my avoidance of impulse purchases, with only one or two minor slip-ups since, not all my purchases are as great as I'd like them to be. The reason for this can't be impulse, that variable is accounted for, but rather a discrepancy between perceived wants and actual wants. I congratulate the advertising industry for successfully playing it's part in this.

Whereas with the impulse purchases of before I would receive the item and go "what was I thinking!", the new needless orders have me wondering how I came to my inflated expectations that are now disappointed.
